# SQC YouTube demo script + shot list — Queen? Never Heard of Her

Working title: **I Tried to Win Chess After Throwing Away My Queen**

Target length: 4-6 minutes.

Goal: make a new viewer understand Side Quest Chess in one sitting: pick a ridiculous quest, play real chess elsewhere, SQC verifies it, proof becomes shareable.

Primary CTA: `Try one stupidly hard side quest at sidequestchess.com`.

## Positioning

- Tone: playful, chess-aware, not childish.
- Promise: “Play normal chess. Complete ridiculous challenges. Brag when it works.”
- Key reassurance: SQC does not need Lichess/Chess.com passwords; it checks public games/usernames.
- Best quest for first demo: **Queen? Never Heard of Her** — win after losing your queen before move 15.

## Full script

### Cold open

**VO:**

“Normal chess says losing your queen before move 15 is a disaster. Side Quest Chess says: congratulations, you may have just unlocked a side quest.”

**On-screen text:**

`Lose queen early. Still win. Become a Certified Queenless Maniac.`

### Intro

**VO:**

“This is Side Quest Chess: chess, but with stupidly hard side quests. You still play real games on Lichess or Chess.com. The difference is that SQC gives you ridiculous optional objectives, then checks your public games to see if you actually pulled them off.”

“Think of it less like training software and more like a chess friend saying, ‘I dare you to win without castling,’ then handing you a tiny coat of arms if you survive.”

### Quest selection

**VO:**

“The quest hub is basically a menu of bad decisions. No Castle Club: win without castling. Pawn Storm Maniac: move a suspicious number of pawns early and still win. Knightmare Mode: deliver mate with a knight. And then there’s today’s quest: Queen? Never Heard of Her.”

**On-screen text:**

`Pick challenge → play elsewhere → SQC checks → proof + badge`

### Queenless rules

**VO:**

“The rules are simple and deeply rude. You have to lose your queen before move 15. Your opponent still has their queen after that moment. The game has to be long enough to count. And, because SQC is cruel but fair, you still have to win.”

“This is not ‘blunder and cope.’ This is ‘blunder and provide documentation.’”

### Account / privacy reassurance

**VO:**

“To make checking work, SQC needs your public Lichess or Chess.com username. It does not need — or ask for — any Lichess or Chess.com passwords. It is looking at public game records, not logging into your chess account.”

“If you just want to browse quests, you can browse. Sign-in matters when you want saved progress, proof, badges, and multiplayer side quests.”

### Gameplay segment

**VO option A — successful game available:**

“Here is the moment the queen leaves the board. This is normally where a responsible person resigns, goes outside, and thinks about choices. Instead, we continue, because the badge demands suffering.”

“From here the mission is not to play beautiful chess. The mission is to become incredibly annoying, keep pieces active, and wait for the opponent to remember they are also capable of blundering.”

**VO option B — no successful game available:**

“I do not have a clean queenless win ready, which is honestly evidence that this quest is doing its job. So I’ll show the checking flow with a recent game and what happens when SQC says: nice try, goblin, but no badge.”

### Verification

**VO:**

“After the game, I come back to Side Quest Chess and check the latest game. SQC reads the public game data and runs the quest rules. Did the queen disappear early enough? Did the opponent still have theirs? Did I win? Was the game eligible?”

“If not, it fails honestly. If yes, you get the receipt.”

### Proof / badge moment

**VO:**

“And this is the fun part: proof. Points, badge, completion state, and a shareable proof scroll. It is intentionally a little ceremonial, because if you win after throwing your queen into the sun, you deserve paperwork.”

**On-screen text:**

`Proof > vibes`

### Multiplayer teaser

**VO:**

“You can also make this social. Send a friend a specific dare, or create a Multiplayer Side Quest where multiple people try the same cursed objective. That is probably the correct way to ruin a chess group chat.”

### CTA

**VO:**

“If this sounds like a terrible idea you want to try, go to sidequestchess.com. Start with No Castle Club if you want a sane first run. Start with Queen? Never Heard of Her if you believe the queen is mostly decorative.”

“Play normal chess. Complete ridiculous challenges. Brag when it works.”
